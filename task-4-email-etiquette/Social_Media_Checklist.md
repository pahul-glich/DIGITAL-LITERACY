# 📱 Social Media Do’s and Don’ts Checklist

Responsible social media use is an important part of digital literacy. As a college student, maintaining a safe, respectful, and professional online presence can positively impact academics, networking, and future career opportunities.

---

## ✅ Do’s

### 1. Think Before You Post
Always check whether your post is respectful, appropriate, and necessary before sharing it publicly.

### 2. Keep Your Profile Professional
Maintain a clean and positive online image that reflects maturity and responsibility.

### 3. Verify Information Before Sharing
Cross-check news, screenshots, and forwarded content before reposting to avoid spreading misinformation.

### 4. Use Privacy and Security Settings
Enable strong passwords, two-factor authentication, and privacy controls to protect your accounts.

### 5. Use Social Media for Learning and Growth
Follow educational pages, professional communities, and useful content that adds value to your academic journey.

---

## ❌ Don’ts

### 1. Don’t Share Personal or Sensitive Information
Avoid posting phone numbers, addresses, OTPs, passwords, financial details, or private conversations.

### 2. Don’t Post Offensive or Harmful Content
Never upload content that is abusive, insulting, discriminatory, or harmful to others.

### 3. Don’t Click Unknown Links
Be cautious with suspicious links, fake giveaways, phishing messages, or unknown attachments.

### 4. Don’t Engage in Online Fights or Cyberbullying
Avoid unnecessary arguments, trolling, public humiliation, or disrespectful behaviour online.

### 5. Don’t Damage Your Digital Reputation
Do not post anything that could negatively affect your academic image, internships, placements, or future opportunities.

---

## 🌐 Final Note
Social media can be a powerful tool when used responsibly. A student’s online presence should reflect awareness, professionalism, and digital responsibility.
